We maintain our gold price forecast of $3,700/toz by year-end and $4,000/toz by mid-2026, despite delayed Fed cuts and lower US recession risk (35% recession odds in the next 12 month vs. 45% prior) for two reasons. 
First, the combined estimated hit from these factors to our mid-2026 forecast is only $15/toz, partly because our terminal Fed funds rate expectation remains unchanged at 3.5-3.75%. 

Second, we now assume a very modest amount of private sector diversification into gold, which offsets the slight hit from the cyclically more benign macro outlook. 
Although the probability-weighted risks to our forecast remain to the upside on net, extreme price upside above our base case now appears less likely in 2025 due to recent trade deals and reduced recession risk.
